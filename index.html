About

Hi! I'm Ismael, a third-year undergrad at the University of Chicago studying Statistics and Computer Science. I'm a member of Professor Ramon Nogueira's lab in the University of Chicago Neuroscience Theory Center. I'm passionate about computational neuroscience and leveraging neurotechnology to improve lives.

My research interests lie at the intersection of neuroscience and machine learning. I believe the success of foundation models in NLP can translate to neural data; over the next decades, models that generalize across tasks and subjects will enable practical BCI applications, from direct machine interfaces to clinical therapies for mental disorders. I'm fascinated by both the science and its potential for impact.

In my spare time, I love playing tennis and volleyball, rock climbing, hiking, and searching for the best chocolate croissant.

Projects

  • EEG

    EEG Monthly arXiv Digest ↗

    Monthly digest tracking EEG foundation model papers on arXiv with an automated pipeline: keyword search, title/abstract triage, and PDF-level LLM summarization.

    492 candidates → 94 accepted through February 2026 · ~30k tokens per accepted paper including triage overhead

    Definition excludes single-task pretrain/fine-tune pipelines and focuses on broad-transfer EEG models. Limitations: arXiv-only coverage, search misses, and possible LLM triage/summary errors.

    View process

  • RallyClip ↗

    Tennis match segmentation software with a CLI and installable GUI. Uses YOLO pose models for feature extraction, CV methods for court detection, and Bi-Directional LSTM for frame-by-frame in-point probability prediction. Designed for free, local use to democratize access to sports analytics tools.

  • Memory Vault ↗

    Chrome extension to locally save browsing history semantically rather than with keywords. Uses embeddings stored in a local vector database for natural language search over your browsing history.
